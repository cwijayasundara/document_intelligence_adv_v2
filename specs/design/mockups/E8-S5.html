What is the management fee structure for this fund?
2:34 PM

The Horizon Equity Partners IV fund has a two-tier management fee structure:

During the Investment Period (first 5 years): The management fee is 2.00% per annum, calculated on committed capital. This is a standard rate for middle-market buyout funds.

Post-Investment Period: The fee steps down to 1.50% per annum, calculated on invested capital (rather than committed capital), which is favorable to limited partners as it reduces fees on uninvested capital.

How does the carried interest waterfall work, and what is the preferred return hurdle?
2:36 PM

The carried interest structure follows a standard private equity distribution waterfall:

Preferred Return (Hurdle Rate): Limited Partners receive an 8% per annum preferred return, compounded annually, on their contributed capital before the General Partner receives any carried interest. This is an industry-standard hurdle rate for PE funds.

Carried Interest: Once the preferred return has been met, the General Partner receives 20% of net profits above the hurdle. The GP has also committed to contributing 2% of aggregate commitments, aligning interests with LPs.

This structure incentivizes the GP to generate returns above the 8% threshold before participating in profits, which is a standard LP protection mechanism.
